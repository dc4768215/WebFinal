Rundown

The conflict between Ukraine and Russia is not a recent affair but something that has been going on since the declaration of their independence from the Soviet Union in 1991. Their separation was a result of Russia’s imploding economy after the Cold War. In an attempt to gain power over surrounding countries by investing lots of money into their military, the state of their economy took a massive hit so countries who didn’t want to be a part of the Soviet Union, one of them being Ukraine, took that as an opportunity to separate as Russia and their crippling economy would not be able to do anything about it. Since then there have been multiple efforts from Russia to try and get Ukraine back from making them elect a puppet president, Viktor Yanukovych, in 2010 to secretly sending Russian military into Ukraine military to fight separatists in 2014. Ukraine’s slow attempts at trying to join NATO, who would safeguard their freedom and security, has always been a lingering threat to Russia and are now taking action by invading their Ukraine starting the 2022 Russian Invasion of Ukraine.
